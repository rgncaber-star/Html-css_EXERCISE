Introduction

Nature and Importance of the study

Cassava, Manihot esculenta Crantz, is a perenial woody shrub with an edible root, which grows in tropical and subtropical areas of the world. it is also called yuca, manioc, mandioca and also known kamoteng kahoy or bilanghoy in local name. Cassava is the basis many products, including food. it is mostly used for human consumption, and also used commercialy for the production of animal feed and starch-based products (IITA, 2009).



The cassava industry in the Philippines is now gaining momentum with the existence of various market opportunities. Cassava is grown not only for human food, but also for starch animal feed and industrial uses, For past twenty years cassava production in the Philippines showed an irregular trend. In the late 1070s cassava production was at its peak. There was an increase of 108% area from 87,420 hectare in 1973 to 181, 770 hectare in 1978, which result in a 356% increase in total volume of production and a 120% increase in a average yeild (FAOST, 2001). The increase in the national average cassava yeild 6.38 tons\ha. (Evangelio, 1998)

The Bureau of Agricultural Statistics (BAS) selected root crops such as cassava, sweet potato, gabi and ubi registered a total production of 16,073 metrics tons. Cssava represents 45.36% of the total production with an average yeild of 1.27 metric tons\hectares (http://smar.lgu-ph.com/econo.htm) (82000).

Objective of the study


1. To determine the growth and yeild performance of different cassava cultivars.

Time and Place of the Study


The study was conducted from April until December 2009 in the upland area located at Brgy. Geraga-an. Calbayog City, SAMAR.

Scoop and Limitation of the Study


This research focused on the growth and yield performance of different cassava cultivars such as, T1 (Makan-1), T2 (rayong-5), T3 (Lakan-1), and T4 (Lakan-2) growth under Brgy. Geraga-an Calbayog City, Samar.
